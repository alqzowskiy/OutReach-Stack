# Research playbook

## What counts as a usable hook
- Specific and verifiable: you can name the exact post, number, launch, or person.
- Recent enough to matter: weeks or a few months old, not a job they left years ago.
- Relevant to your ask: a hook that connects to why you are writing beats a random fact.
- Something the recipient would recognize about themselves in one read.

## What does not count
- "I see you work in tech" or "as a leader in your space": generic, skip it.
- A compliment with no detail behind it ("loved your work").
- Anything you are guessing at. If you cannot point to a source, do not use it.

## Red lines
- Never fabricate a detail to make the message warmer. A wrong hook is worse than none.
- Never use personal or sensitive information: home address, family, health, anything that is not clearly professional and public.
- Never write the recipient's details to disk, into the repo, or into the voice profile. Research stays in the conversation.
- Public and professional only: their company site, their posts, their talks, press coverage. If it starts to feel like surveillance, stop.

## When research comes up dry
Say so directly. Tell the user the outreach will be research-blind and recommend a short, honest message that does not pretend to personalization. A clean cold ask beats a fake-personal one.
