# Cold email: channel rules

These are physical constraints of the channel, not style preferences. A draft that breaks them does not get shown.

## Subject line
- 3 to 6 words. It competes in a crowded inbox preview, so it earns the open on its own.
- Make it specific to the recipient or the ask. "question about your on-call rotation" beats "quick question".
- No clickbait, no fake "Re:", no all-caps, no emoji.
- Sentence case or lowercase reads like a person, not a campaign.
- Good: "Maria suggested I email you", "cut your CI flakes", "15 min on your hiring crunch".
- Bad: "Revolutionizing Your Workflow", "Partnership Opportunity", "Touching base", "Following up".

## First line
- The first line shows in the inbox preview next to the subject, so it is part of the open, not a warm-up.
- Do not spend it on "I hope this finds you well" or "My name is X and I am the founder of Y". Lead with the hook, the shared contact, or the specific reason you are writing.

## Length
- 50 to 125 words in the body. Shorter usually wins. A cold email that needs scrolling does not get read.
- One idea. If you are explaining background, you are too long: cut it, or save it for a second touch.

## The ask
- Exactly one CTA. Two asks means zero replies.
- Put the ask inside the first third of the email, not the last line.
- Make it a single easy action: a specific time window, a yes or no, a reply with a date, an intro forward. Not "let me know your thoughts" or "would love to connect".
- Lower the cost of yes. "Tuesday or Thursday at 2pm your time?" beats "let me know when works for you".

## Links and formatting
- At most one link, and only if it directly serves the ask. Several links read like a newsletter and can trip spam filters.
- No images, no tracking pixels, no attachments on a first cold email.
- Plain paragraphs, two or three sentences each. No bullet lists inside a 90 word email.

## Deliverability (what sends a first email to spam)
- Attachments, images, or tracking pixels on a cold first touch.
- Multiple links, link shorteners, or a bare URL as the whole CTA.
- Spam-trigger phrasing: "free", "guarantee", "act now", "limited time", all-caps, rows of exclamation marks.
- A mismatch between the display name and the sending domain.
- Keep it text, keep it short, keep it to one link, and most of this takes care of itself.

## Signature
- Name, one line of who you are, and a single way to verify you (a real company or site). Nothing more.

## Warm vs cold
- **Cold (no prior contact):** you owe the recipient a reason you are in their inbox. The first line has to establish relevance fast.
- **Warm (some prior contact or a shared connection):** name the connection in the first line ("Maria suggested I reach out"), then move to the ask. You can be shorter and less formal, but the one-CTA rule still holds.
